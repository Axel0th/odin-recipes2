Slow-Baked Sea Bream with Tahini Sauce and Pine Nuts

Description

This creamy and aromatic dish is a reflection on the flavours used within Palestine. The nutty tahini combines wonderfully with the butter, garlic and lemon to bring out the best from such a tasty and tender white fish.

Ingredients

  • 2 x 400g Sea Bream Cleaned, Gutted and De-scaled
  • 100g Pine Nuts
  • 3 Large Garlic Cloves
  • 1 Medium Lemon
  • 1 Tbsp Tahini
  • 50g Salted Butter
  • 1 large Handful of Parsley
  • 3 Tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 Tsp Harissa

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 160 degrees Celsius.
  2. In a food processor, combine garlic, tahini, oil, lemon juice, parsley, harissa, butter, and 50g of pine nuts. Blitz until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  3. Smother the insides and outsides of the fish with the creamy mixture, making sure to massage the sauce all over.
  4. Bake the fish in the oven for 25 minutes until the sauce is thicker and beginning to caramelize.
  5. Just before the fish is ready, dry fry the remaining pine nuts in a frying pan until golden brown and sprinkle them over the fish.
  6. This dish tastes great with salad, rice, or couscous, and a dash of plain yogurt and lemon. It's recommended to serve the fish on a bed of fresh spinach.
  7. Tip: Sea bream is ideally purchased from a market or fishmonger as a wild-caught fish, with a minimum weight of 400g per fish when possible.
